Trabablas Interchange: Revolutionizing Harare’s Traffic Infrastructure

The Trabablas Interchange is Zimbabwe’s largest road infrastructure development project to date. Strategically situated in Harare, this multi-level interchange is designed to eliminate congestion at one of the busiest and most critical junctions in the country. The project is a flagship of national infrastructural transformation and a key enabler of economic growth through efficient logistics.

Client:

Government of Zimbabwe

Location:

Harare, Zimbabwe

Project Year:

2021

Status:

Completed

Duration:

4 Years

Value:

USD $88 Million

The Trabablas Interchange replaces a chaotic and hazardous roundabout system with a modern, grade-separated cloverleaf design. It features multiple flyovers, underpasses, and slip roads that allow uninterrupted traffic flow across major arterial roads including Simon Mazorodze, Chitungwiza Road, and High Glen Road. Since completion, it has significantly reduced travel time, improved road safety, and enhanced logistical efficiency for domestic and regional traffic.

“This transformative project is a major milestone in Zimbabwe’s commitment to modernising infrastructure and building for the future.”

Fossil Contracting executed the project under the Emergency Road Rehabilitation Programme (ERRP2) and brought world-class engineering, project management, and construction capabilities to this endeavor. Leveraging a comprehensive plant fleet and experienced personnel, Fossil Contracting delivered works including excavation, concrete bridge construction, road surfacing, retaining walls, and stormwater drainage infrastructure.

Upon completion, the Trabablas Interchange became a model of excellence in Southern African road engineering and a symbol of national pride, facilitating smoother transit and catalyzing economic activity across sectors.

Harare–Chirundu Road Upgrading & Widening Project

As part of the North–South Corridor, the Harare–Chirundu Road is a vital regional link connecting Zimbabwe to Zambia and further north to the Democratic Republic of Congo. This upgrading and widening project is key to enhancing trade efficiency, regional integration, and national transport capacity under SADC infrastructure development goals.

Client:

Government of Zimbabwe

Location:

Harare to Chirundu

Project Year:

2022

Status:

Ongoing

Duration:

5 Years

Value:

USD $360 Million

The project entails dualisation, road widening, rehabilitation of damaged sections, and the upgrading of bridges and culverts to improve road safety and traffic capacity. Fossil Contracting is responsible for full construction services along designated segments, including asphalt surfacing, concrete drainage systems, and road markings. The project also supports regional trade and logistics by creating a smoother, safer, and more durable roadway for heavy trucks and passenger vehicles.

“A critical lifeline for Zimbabwe’s economy and a pillar of SADC connectivity.”

Fossil’s work includes removing aged infrastructure, re-compacting subgrades, and constructing new layers of road base and surfacing. All construction activities are executed under strict safety, quality, and environmental control standards. This section of the corridor will drastically cut travel time and costs while reducing vehicle operating expenses and accidents.

The Harare–Chirundu upgrade is a shining example of public-private partnership in infrastructure development and showcases Fossil Contracting’s competence in delivering mega-scale, transformative projects that fuel national and regional development.

Construction of Defe Dopota Dam

The Defe Dopota Dam project represents a strategic investment in sustainable water infrastructure aimed at improving agricultural productivity, climate resilience, and water security in the Mashonaland West province of Zimbabwe. Fossil Contracting was engaged to deliver comprehensive civil engineering solutions, from earthworks to final dam wall construction.

Client:

Government of Zimbabwe

Location:

Defe Dopota, Mashonaland West

Project Year:

2021

Status:

Completed

Duration:

18 Months

Value:

USD $13 Million

The scope of work included excavation, bulk earth moving, embankment compaction, spillway construction, concrete outlet works, and auxiliary structures. The dam is designed to serve both irrigation and potable water needs in the region, helping local farmers and surrounding communities thrive in a climate-vulnerable environment.

“Water security is at the heart of national development, and this project empowers communities to flourish.”

Fossil Contracting deployed a medium-sized fleet of dozers, excavators, and tippers for efficient dam shaping and earth compaction. The team delivered high-standard civil works, including structural concrete, dam liners, drainage control, and the installation of control valves and pipes.

This successful delivery demonstrates Fossil’s growing specialization in dam construction and underscores its commitment to agricultural infrastructure development, climate adaptation, and sustainable engineering practices across Zimbabwe.

Great Dyke Investments (GDI) Civil Works Project

Fossil Contracting was awarded a major contract for site civil works at the Great Dyke Investments platinum project in Darwendale, Zimbabwe. This initiative is one of the largest platinum mining investments in the country and aims to establish a world-class integrated platinum group metals (PGM) mining and processing operation.

Client:

Great Dyke Investments

Location:

Darwendale, Zimbabwe

Project Year:

2020

Status:

Completed (Phase 1)

Duration:

24 Months

Value:

USD $25 Million

The scope of work included extensive bulk earthworks, road construction, stormwater drainage, and concrete works for key plant infrastructure. As part of enabling works for the processing plant and support facilities, Fossil delivered access roads, foundations, platforms, culverts, and stormwater protection structures.

“This project reinforces Zimbabwe’s platinum sector growth and demonstrates Fossil’s capability in mining infrastructure.”

The work required precision in geo-technical stabilisation, compaction of engineered fill, and preparation of critical areas for mechanical and structural installations. Safety and environmental compliance were observed at all stages in accordance with international mining and construction standards.

With the successful delivery of this contract, Fossil Contracting cemented its reputation as a reliable partner in large-scale mining infrastructure, helping pave the way for one of Zimbabwe’s most ambitious mineral sector investments.

Harare–Beitbridge Highway Rehabilitation Project

The Harare–Beitbridge Highway is Zimbabwe’s busiest and most strategic trade route, linking the country to South Africa and the wider SADC region. Under the Emergency Road Rehabilitation Programme (ERRP), Fossil Contracting was tasked with rehabilitating key sections of this critical highway to modern, durable standards.

Client:

Government of Zimbabwe

Location:

Harare to Beitbridge

Project Year:

2021

Status:

Completed

Duration:

24 Months

Value:

USD $90 Million

The scope of works included full-depth road reconstruction, resurfacing, widening, and drainage upgrades across numerous kilometres. Fossil Contracting employed modern construction equipment, high-quality bitumen, and asphalt mixes to ensure the road met regional trunk road standards.

“A flagship infrastructure project that strengthens regional logistics and stimulates national economic growth.”

The Harare–Beitbridge upgrade improves travel efficiency, enhances freight handling, and reduces wear and tear on vehicles. The rehabilitation also involved installation of road signage, culverts, and traffic safety systems. The project created hundreds of local jobs and transferred key civil engineering skills.

The project is a testament to Fossil Contracting’s capacity to execute national priority works with speed, quality, and professionalism, making a lasting impact on the country’s transport infrastructure.
